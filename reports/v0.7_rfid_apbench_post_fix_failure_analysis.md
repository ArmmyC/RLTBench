## Public Expected Behavior

The public task asks for `ap_006_wakeup_edge_filter` with ports `clk`, `rst_n`, `enable`, `signal_in`, and `wake_pulse`.

At a high level, the expected behavior is:

- active-low reset clears internal state and drives `wake_pulse` low
- when `enable` is low, `wake_pulse` is low and prior input history is forgotten
- when enabled, a rising edge of `signal_in` produces a one-cycle `wake_pulse`
- holding `signal_in` high must not retrigger the pulse

The reference implements a registered previous-input state and a registered pulse output. The testbench enables the design, drives an initial low-to-high input transition, checks for a pulse after the active clock edge, checks that a held-high input does not retrigger, checks low input, checks a second rising edge, and checks that disabled mode suppresses the pulse.

The public prompt, reference, and testbench agree on the edge-filter contract. The testbench expectation is aligned with the reference's clocked one-cycle pulse behavior.

## Compile And Synthesis Failure Classification

Sample 2 is classified as:

- `syntax_or_compile_construct_error`
- `incomplete_or_malformed_candidate`
- `testbench_expectation_not_met` as a downstream gate consequence

The candidate extracted successfully and had the requested top module, but local compile inspection found a malformed edge-detection construct. Because compile failed, simulation correctness and synthesis scoring could not produce a valid candidate result. This is candidate-generation evidence, not endpoint, extractor, benchmark asset, or tool-health evidence.

## Simulation Failure Classification

Samples 1 and 3 are classified as:

- `edge_filter_semantics_mismatch`
- `wakeup_pulse_width_mismatch`
- `state_update_timing_mismatch`
- `testbench_expectation_not_met`

Both candidates compiled and synthesized, but failed the public functional simulation. Sanitized local inspection indicates the generated pulse behavior was not available as the expected one-cycle registered pulse after the relevant clock edge. The failure therefore concerns candidate implementation semantics for edge detection and pulse timing, not response reliability or synthesis availability.

## Benchmark Asset Decision

Keep benchmark assets unchanged. No concrete public benchmark bug was found in `task.yaml`, `prompt.md`, `reference.sv`, or `testbench.sv`. The public assets consistently define a one-cycle pulse on enabled rising edges of `signal_in`, no retrigger while held high, and disabled reset of prior input state.

## Fine-Tuning Readiness Decision

Fine-tuning remains out of scope and is not recommended. Three public/synthetic invalid rows on one task are useful evaluation evidence, not a training dataset or sufficient basis for model-weight or adapter work.

## Recommended Next Bounded Direction

Recommend exactly one bounded direction: `v0.7_rfid_apbench_release_closeout_spec`.

Rationale: the remaining `ap_006` invalid pattern is sufficiently classified as candidate implementation failure, while response reliability and benchmark assets are not implicated. The baseline should be closed out with the known `ap_006` limitation documented rather than triggering prompt edits, runner changes, broad reruns, model comparison, private evaluation, max-token changes, or fine-tuning.
